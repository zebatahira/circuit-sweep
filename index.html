How Circuit Sweep Works

A neon rewiring of the classic 1989 logic puzzle.

Objective

The board hides a set number of mines beneath a grid of circuit nodes. Reveal every node that isn't a mine without triggering one. Numbers left behind on cleared nodes tell you how many mines are hiding in the eight neighboring cells.

Controls

  • Left click / tap a hidden cell to reveal it. That's the only control — there's no flagging to manage.
  • Your very first click is always safe — mines are placed only after that click, and never under it or its immediate neighbors.

Reading the grid

Each revealed number is color-coded by value, just like the mine count it represents:

11 mine
22 mines
33 mines
44 mines

Winning & losing

Clear every safe cell on the board and the console flashes green — you win. Click a mine and the whole field detonates in red — game over, and every mine is revealed so you can see where they were hiding.

Difficulty

  • Beginner — 9×9 grid, 10 mines.
  • Intermediate — 16×16 grid, 40 mines.
  • Expert — 30×16 grid, 99 mines.
